The tablet, usually abbreviated to the tablet, is a mobile device, usually equipped with a mobile operating system, a touch screen LCD, and a rechargeable battery in one thin, flat package. Tablet PCs, which use computers, work with other PCs, but lack some other I / O capabilities. Modern panels are very much like modern smartphones. The only differences are that tablet devices are relatively larger than smart phones, with screens measuring 7 inches (18 cm) or larger, measured diagonally, and may not support access to a cellular network.

The touch screen is turned on by finger or pen gestures instead of the mouse, trackpad, and keyboard for large computers. Laptops can be classified according to the presence of physical keyboards and their appearance. Two types of tablets, the "tablet" and the "brochure," do not contain physical keyboards and usually accept text and other inputs using a virtual keyboard that appears on their touch screens. To make up for the absence of a physical keyboard, most tablets can connect to independent physical keyboards via Bluetooth or USB; 2-in-1 PCs contain keyboards that are distinct from tablets.
